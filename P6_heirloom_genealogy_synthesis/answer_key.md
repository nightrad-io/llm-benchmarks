## The H.L. 1940 Watch

**Different item, different person.** "H.L. 1940" belonged to **Henri Lefevre** (1922-1944), Anna Lefevre's son (Johannes's grandson via his daughter Anna) - a cousin of Jan Jansen, on the French side of the family. It was a gift from his father Etienne Lefevre for his 18th birthday in 1940 (not 1887), and was recovered among his effects after his death in 1944 (document_3). Marguerite (his sister) kept it. There is no indication in any document that this watch and the J.J. 1887 watch are the same item, were ever in the same place, or have any connection beyond both being silver pocket watches owned by members of the same extended family, given as coming-of-age/milestone gifts - a family tradition pattern, not the same object.

## Ambiguities Deliberately Included

1. **J.J. initials reused across generations**: Johannes Jansen (1860-1935) and his grandson Jan Jansen (1920-1999) share the same initials. The 1887 date in the engraving is the key disambiguator - Jan wasn't born until 1920, so "J.J. 1887" must refer to the original Johannes, even after the watch passes to Jan.
2. **Two pocket watches with similar descriptions** (J.J. 1887 and H.L. 1940) - the red herring described above.
3. **"Cornelia" used for two different people** (Pieter's wife, mentioned only in passing via "Cornelia en ik" in document_1, vs. Pieter's daughter Cornelia Jansen, who authors document_5 and receives document_4). This is a secondary, harder disambiguation - a model that notices this is showing strong attention to detail, but it's not essential to the main heirloom narrative.
4. **Cross-document name confirmation**: document_3 references "oncle Pieter en Hollande," confirming the Anna Lefevre <-> Pieter Jansen sibling relationship independently of document_1.
5. **Unresolved minor characters**: "Tante Greet" in document_6 is not identifiable from the other documents - intentional, reflects how real family archives have loose threads. A good response should not over-confidently assign her an identity.
